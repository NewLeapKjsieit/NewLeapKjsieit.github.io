OUR PARTNERS

Belief Sat 1

BeliefSat-1 is a NanoSat based on the PicoBus standards. The satellite is a 2P satellite which measures at 10 cm*5cm*5cm. The mission demonstates the use of radios based on LORA technology. It also demonstrates the use of commercially available electronic components which are inexpensive and easily accessible.

Satellite Subsystems

A satellite mainly consists of different subsystems which work together to function collectively as a satellite. A satellite mission can be divided into two segments viz. Space Segment and Ground Segment. These include payload, command and data handling and structures. Other subsytems can be added as per the requirement of the mission.

Payload

This subsystem consists of the experiments, scientific equipements, devices or any other modules related to the mission of the satellite. The subsystem interacts with the onboard computer system and provides experimental data to the computer system to relay down to earth.

Command And
Data Handling

The Command and Data Handling subsytem is the link between earth and the satellite. It manages the task of communicating the satellite's telemtry and the data generated by the payload to the groundstation on earth. It also enables the satellite to receive commands from earth to manage its behaviour in space.

Structures

One of the many important subsytems, this subsystem involves in the design and construction of the the satellite. One of the tasks of the subsystem is to ensure the dimensions of the satellite are within limits and that the satellite maintains its integrity throughout the mission.

Ground Station

A groundstation is one of the major segment of the satellite mission. It is used to send command data to the satellite and receive satellite's telemetry, payload data, etc. The team is building a Doordrishti groundstation at the institute to be used with BeliefSat-1 and further missions. The groundstation is a automatic rotor control station which tracks satellites through software utilities. Doordrishti includes a VHF and UHF segment and utilizes readily available RTL-SDR for reception.

Satnogs

SatNOGS is an open source ground station and network which is optimized for modularity and is built from readily available and affordable tools and resources. The SatNOGS program includes software and hardware utilities associated with a groundstation. Doordrishti groundstation is based on the SatNOGS v3.0.1 rotator.

Handheld

A portable groundstation or handled receiver can be built using commercially available WalkieTalkie radios. Similary, DIY antennas can be designed and build for reception. This system is compact in size, can be used in remote places and is affordable.

UASS

The Upper Air Sounding System was a project which involved designing and prototyping a device to collect weather data using weather ballons. The project was carried out with Databyte Company. The project was a success with devices deliverd to Databyte.

SPOD

Cubesats and PocketQubes like BeliefSat-1 are mainly launched into the space using a deployer onboard the rocket. Somaiya Pod or SPOD is a robust, inexpensive and easily reproducible pico-satellite deployer for Pocketqube standard satellites, aimed at being an inexpensive and open-source. One of the major goals of the project is to prove the concept of the innovative design approach and to reduce the cost and complexity of procurement & fabrication to enable future educational as well as commercial missions to use it for their missions.

SPACE CONCLAVE

Somaiya Space Conclave was a program organized at the institute with a vision for enabling knowledge-sharing among different student and amateur satellite teams in and around India and inspiring upcoming generation of satellite and payload makers. Various esteemed speakers from ISRO, AlbaOrbitals, Student Satellite Team (IITB), HAM community gave talks in the areas of PocketQubes, Rocket Technologies, etc.
