Projects @NLI

BELIEFSAT-1


The BeliefSat is a 2p standard PocketQube Nano Satellite being developed by the students of K J Somaiya Institute of Engineering and Information Technology, Sion, Mumbai. It is an Amateur Radio satellite accepted for launch under ISRO's PS4 Orbital Platform Programme along with the deployment mechanism named SomaiyaPod.


The team aims to demonstrate the deployment of BeliefSat from the SomaiyaPOD. We also aim to perform functions like Antenna Deployment, Digipeating and Voice-repeating.

DOOR-DRISHTI


The team is also developing an Automated Ground Station system for BeleifSat-1. The main aim of developing this system is to make it cost effective and flexible platform for tracking “Low Earth Orbital Satellites”.


The system consists of a Yagi Uda antenna working in the 2m HAM band, which would be mount on the antenna rotator mechanism. The “Base Transceiver System” would then communicate with the satellite and display the received data on the web server.

INDRAVANI


Under the Make in India Initiative, the team got an opportunity to develop an Indigenous Upper Air Sounding System. This project is in collaboration with Databyte Services and Systems, mentored by Mr. Vishram Bapat (Director, Databyte Services and Systems).


The project aims to design a Radiosonde, which is a device that measures the weather parameters, like temperature, humidity, wind speed, wind direction, location and humidity in the “Upper Air” region at the heights of around 40000 ft. Also, the team is developing a receiver station which will receive all the data from the Radiosonde during its flight ascent.
